Economy Game 2.0 - Teaching Innovation
(with Martin Strobel and Chris van der Lans) [GitHub project]

We develop a market simulation tool (oTree, JS, HTML) enabling students to live stylized market settings. Via a smartphone-compatible, browser based platform students trade virtual goods with their student fellows subject to manifold market environments. Supplementing conventional teaching, such first hand-experience opens a different route to understanding of canonical concepts in microeconomics such as consumer theory, producer theory, partial equilibrium, general equilibrium and welfare.

We are thankful for financial support by the Maastricht University Education Institute andEDLAB .
